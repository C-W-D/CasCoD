Q: The following paragraphs each describe a set of three objects arranged in a fixed order. The statements are logically consistent within each paragraph. In a golf tournament, there were three golfers: Mel, Ada, and Ana. Mel finished last. Ana finished second.
Options:
(A) Mel finished first
(B) Ada finished first
(C) Ana finished first

Right Response: (1) Mel finished last: "(first) ? ? ? Mel (last)".
(2) Ana finished second: "(first) ? ? Ana ? Mel (last)".
(3) Combining (1) and (2) we get the following ordering: "(first) ? ? Ana Mel (last)".
According to this ordering, the person who finished first (the one at the top of this list) is Ada.
Ada finished first. Therefore, the answer is (B).

Wrong Response: (1) Mel finished last: "(first) ? ? ? Mel (last)".
(2) Ana finished second: "(first) ? ? Ana ? Mel (last)".
Based on these statements, we can deduce that Ana finished first, as she is the only one left to be placed in the first position.
Ana finished first. Therefore, the answer is (C).

(B) The hawk is the second from the left

The hawk is the second from the left. Therefore, the answer is (B).
